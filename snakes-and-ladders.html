About Snakes and Ladders

Snakes and Ladders is a classic board game that has entertained families for generations. Originating in ancient India as "Moksha Patam," this game of luck has evolved into a worldwide favorite that combines simplicity with excitement.

The game is played on a grid of squares, typically numbered from 1 to 100. Players roll a dice to move their tokens along the board, with the goal of reaching the final square before their opponents. The twist comes from the ladders, which provide shortcuts up the board, and the snakes, which send players sliding back down.

Our online version brings this timeless classic to your digital devices, allowing you to enjoy Snakes and Ladders anytime, anywhere, without needing a physical board or dice.

How to Play

  1. Starting the Game:

    All players start at square 1, which is typically in the bottom left corner of the board.

  2. Taking Turns:

    Players take turns rolling the dice and moving their token the corresponding number of squares.

  3. Climbing Ladders:

    If a player lands on a square with the bottom of a ladder, they immediately climb to the square at the top of that ladder.

  4. Sliding Down Snakes:

    If a player lands on a square with the head of a snake, they must slide down to the square at the tail of that snake.

  5. Winning the Game:

    The first player to reach the final square (usually 100) wins the game. Note that you must roll the exact number needed to land on the final square.

History of Snakes and Ladders

Snakes and Ladders has a rich history dating back to the 13th century in India, where it was known as "Moksha Patam" or "Gyan Chaupar." The game was originally designed to teach moral lessons, with ladders representing virtues and snakes representing vices.

The British brought the game to England in the late 19th century, where it was commercialized and renamed "Snakes and Ladders." The moral elements were removed, transforming it into the pure game of chance we know today.

Despite its simplicity, the game's perfect balance of luck, anticipation, and dramatic reversals of fortune has ensured its popularity for centuries across cultures worldwide.
